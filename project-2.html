Mexican Dine Web

Mexican Dine Web is a responsive restaurant site designed to showcase a modern dining experience through clean, high-impact visuals and intuitive navigation. The project focuses on presenting high-resolution menu items and happy hour specials in a way that balances vibrant photography with clear, readable typography. By implementing a seamless mobile-first layout and a structured appetizer grid, the site provides potential diners with an effortless way to explore the menu and book a table from any device.

Building Mexican Dine

IXD 108, 2026

The Mexican Dine Web project is a responsive restaurant website I designed and built using WordPress. The process began with a quick mock-up in Figma, where I planned the layout, user flow, and overall visual direction of the site. This step helped me quickly explore ideas and establish a clear structure before development. I also created a simple logo early in the process to define the brand identity and ensure consistency across all pages. From typography to color choices, I aimed to create a clean and modern look that would reflect a welcoming dining experience.

Building Mexican Dine

IXD 108, 2026

After finalizing the concept, I moved into building the website in WordPress, focusing on usability, responsiveness, and clean design. The site includes four main pages: a home page to introduce the restaurant and highlight key features, an about page to share the story and concept behind the brand, a menu page to showcase food offerings in a clear and visually engaging way, and a booking page that allows users to easily reserve a table. Throughout the build, I prioritized intuitive navigation and accessibility across devices. The final result is a polished, user-friendly website that balances strong visuals with practical functionality.
